Dissertation Projects

During my time in University, I have completed two dissertation projects. These both represent my largest pieces of work and demonstrate best my ability to organise, plan, and prioritise my time effectively to achieve a high standard of work.

In the final-year of my undergraduate program, there was an opportunity to complete a research placement with a partner company, which would involve a blend of practical work experience alongside the completion of a dissertation project. I was one of six students academically eligible to take this module, however the work experience element was cancelled due to the pandemic. My undergrad dissertation was an investigation into staff retention rates for the partner organisation, for which I designed a survey through Qualtrics to analyse quantitative data from over 80 participants, receiving a first-class grade. My work with statistics continued into my Master's programme, which you can view here.

My Master's dissertation was my most intensive and challenging project to date, yet it was my most enjoyable. I completed an analysis of Welsh afforestation policy and produced a report assessing how it has evolved to be able to satisfy current government tree planting targets. I utilised semi-structured interviews with relevant stakeholders to support my findings from my policy analysis. I received a merit for my efforts for which one of my markers noted that "there is an assured skill here in writing a policy report that is quite impressive". This is my proudest piece of work to date, but I have written a variety of reports that I believe are great at further showcasing my written language, research, and report writing skills
